# Factions and Roles

## Purpose
This document defines the major power groups, player-facing roles, and social layers that govern access, authority, and opportunity within the Maze civilization.

## Social Layers (Strata)
The world is structured by **Strata**, determined by legal access and synthesis capability.

- **Directive Strata:** strategic governance, licensing, and policy enforcement.
- **Operational Strata:** logistics, mapping, fabrication, and contract execution.
- **Service Strata:** labor, maintenance, escort, and extraction support.
- **Fringe Populations:** unlicensed actors operating outside formal protections.

These layers shape what players can legally do, whom they can work for, and how quickly they can advance.

## Major Factions

### 1) The Cartograph Assembly
**Function:** mapping authority and route certification.  
**Power Source:** validated sector intelligence and navigation standards.  
**View of the Maze:** a system to be measured and stabilized.

Influence:
- controls official map updates,
- certifies safe corridors for higher-tier travel,
- can raise or lower the value of discoveries by verifying (or refusing) route legitimacy.

Player relationship:
- offers reconnaissance contracts,
- rewards high-fidelity route data,
- grants faster Access Tier advancement for reliable navigators.

### 2) The Weave Consortium
**Function:** synthesis infrastructure and recipe governance.  
**Power Source:** workshop networks, component patents, and process doctrine.  
**View of the Maze:** a living input system for controlled transformation.

Influence:
- standardizes synthesis workflows across Anchors,
- monopolizes certain high-order recipes,
- determines which tools can be legally produced or traded.

Player relationship:
- provides crafting progression paths,
- grants prototype programs to trusted agents,
- can restrict advancement if the player bypasses sanctioned channels.

### 3) The Tier Tribunal
**Function:** legal authority over Access Tiers and enforcement protocols.  
**Power Source:** charter law, licensing records, and punitive force.  
**View of the Maze:** a strategic asset requiring strict hierarchy.

Influence:
- sets entry thresholds by sector class,
- issues sanctions for unauthorized expeditions,
- brokers emergency powers during sector instability.

Player relationship:
- offers compliance and arbitration missions,
- can clear or escalate violations,
- becomes critical in high-tier political story arcs.

### 4) The Outerline Compact
**Function:** coalition of fringe settlements and independent operators.  
**Power Source:** informal routes, salvage networks, and distributed trust.  
**View of the Maze:** a contested commons, not an institutional monopoly.

Influence:
- maintains unregistered passage chains,
- circulates unsanctioned tools and intelligence,
- pressures formal factions by enabling parallel economies.

Player relationship:
- provides high-risk, high-reward contracts,
- unlocks alternative progression when formal access is blocked,
- offers pathways to challenge existing social order.

## Player Roles Over Time
The player may specialize or hybridize across roles:

- **Pathfinder:** prioritizes exploration, route discovery, and hazard intelligence.
- **Synthesist:** focuses on Weave mastery, tool innovation, and resource conversion.
- **Broker:** navigates faction politics, contracts, and reputation economies.
- **Warden-Runner:** balances enforcement and frontier autonomy in contested sectors.

Role identity should emerge from repeated decisions, not a one-time class lock.

## Power and Access Distribution
Power is distributed through three coupled systems:

1. **Legal Access (Tier Tribunal):** who may enter where.
2. **Technical Capability (Weave Consortium):** who can transform findings into durable advantage.
3. **Information Legitimacy (Cartograph Assembly):** whose discoveries become accepted truth.

The Outerline Compact disrupts all three by operating in parallel.

Design implication: meaningful progression requires players to engage with all power channels, whether through compliance, negotiation, or resistance.

## Expansion Hooks
- Cross-faction crisis events that temporarily reshape Access Tier law.
- Faction-specific endgame reputations that influence player-created level curation rights.
- Multi-anchor governance conflicts where players can permanently shift regional control norms.
