# World Background

## Purpose
This document defines the origin logic of the world, why the Maze became civilization’s center, and where the player enters that order.

## Origin: The First Compression
Long before the current age, fragmented city-states competed over finite land, energy, and archival technology. During that era, an engineered planetary event known in surviving records as **the First Compression** folded terrain into recursive corridors and vertical chambers. What remained was **The Maze**: a persistent, shifting superstructure layered across the old world.

The Compression did not erase history; it trapped history in spatial knots. Ruins, research vaults, transport arteries, and ecological zones were woven into Maze sectors. This produced a civilization that no longer expands outward, but downward and inward.

## Why the Maze Is Central to Civilization
The Maze became civilization’s foundation for three reasons:

### 1) Resource Asymmetry
Surface settlements cannot sustain growth alone. Advanced materials, energy catalysts, and forgotten schematics are concentrated in deeper Maze sectors. Prosperity depends on controlled extraction.

### 2) Knowledge Density
The deeper sectors preserve pre-Compression archives and machine remnants. Political power depends on who can interpret and operationalize this knowledge.

### 3) Access Governance
Because depth equals leverage, access is regulated through **Access Tiers**. Institutions, factions, and families define social standing by who can enter which sectors, with what authorization, and under whose protection.

In short: the Maze is not a dungeon beside civilization; it *is* the engine of civilization.

## Social Hierarchy: The Strata System
Society is organized into **Strata**, each shaped by Maze access and synthesis capability.

- **High Strata (Directive Houses / Council Networks):** control policy, licensing, and strategic routes. They govern Access Tier law and long-range planning.
- **Middle Strata (Guilds / Contract Networks):** manage logistics, synthesis workshops, mapping, and transport. They broker opportunity but answer upward.
- **Low Strata (Labor Enclaves / Unlicensed Settlers):** perform hazardous support work near fringe sectors and compete for sponsorship to move upward.
- **Unregistered Fringe:** undocumented populations living beyond formal Anchors. They survive through informal routes, salvage economies, and illicit synthesis.

Movement between Strata is possible but difficult; advancement requires demonstrable Maze value, political patronage, or strategic discoveries.

## Player Starting Position
The player begins as a minimally credentialed expedition candidate attached to a peripheral **Anchor**. They possess limited tools, no durable influence, and provisional entry rights to shallow sectors.

They are visible enough to be used, but not powerful enough to refuse terms.

## Long-Term Motivation
The player’s motivation evolves from survival to authorship:

1. **Stability:** secure resources, survive expeditions, and establish synthesis competence.
2. **Recognition:** earn sponsorship, climb Access Tiers, and gain agency in faction politics.
3. **Leverage:** discover systemic truths about the First Compression and the Weave.
4. **Authorship:** shape the Maze’s future by opening routes, setting policy influence, and enabling player-created sectors/levels as recognized frontier zones.

The long arc is not simply “reach the center.” It is to transform from a controlled participant in the system into a co-architect of the world’s next phase.

## Design Implications
- Narrative progression should track increased Access Tier authority, not only combat difficulty.
- Major story beats should reveal that social power and Maze knowledge are inseparable.
- Endgame framing should support persistent expansion, including formally integrated player-created levels.
