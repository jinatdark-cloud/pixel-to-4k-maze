# Core Gameplay Loop

## Purpose
This document defines the practical gameplay loop for a semi-open-world maze adventure, with emphasis on how exploration and synthesis drive long-term growth.

## Loop Summary
The core loop follows a repeatable sequence:

1. **Prepare at Anchor**
2. **Explore Maze Sector**
3. **Extract and Return**
4. **Synthesize and Upgrade**
5. **Unlock New Access and Objectives**
6. **Re-enter at Higher Risk / Higher Reward**

Each cycle deepens player capability while expanding strategic options.

## Step-by-Step Loop Design

### 1) Prepare at Anchor
At Anchors, players:
- select expedition goals (resource-focused, map-focused, contract-focused),
- equip tools and traversal options,
- choose faction contracts that alter risk/reward,
- review Access Tier limits for currently legal sector entry.

Design intent: preparation is meaningful planning, not menu filler.

### 2) Explore Maze Sector
In-sector gameplay centers on:
- navigation through layered paths,
- hazard reading and route adaptation,
- encounter resolution (environmental, systemic, social),
- discovery of components, relic fragments, and route data.

Design intent: exploration should reward observation, memory, and informed improvisation.

### 3) Extract and Return
Players must decide when to continue pushing depth and when to extract. Extraction locks in expedition gains; overextension risks partial or total loss depending on current systems.

Design intent: tension comes from commitment under uncertainty.

### 4) Synthesize and Upgrade
At Anchor workshops, players use **Synthesis** through **The Weave** to convert raw findings into progression assets:
- tool upgrades,
- sector keys,
- traversal modifiers,
- signal beacons,
- specialized contract utilities.

Synthesis is not a side system; it is the conversion layer between exploration and durable power.

### 5) Unlock New Access and Objectives
Upgrades and reputation gains increase **Access Tier** eligibility. New tiers open:
- deeper sectors,
- faction-gated routes,
- advanced contracts,
- higher-order synthesis recipes.

Design intent: progression should feel institutional and systemic, not purely numerical.

### 6) Re-enter at Higher Risk / Higher Reward
New routes introduce harder conditions, rarer components, and politically sensitive discoveries. The loop repeats with broader strategic possibilities and stronger narrative stakes.

Design intent: difficulty growth should be coupled with world recontextualization.

## How Synthesis Connects to Progression
Synthesis links all major axes of growth:

- **Mechanical growth:** better tools and mobility expand viable playstyles.
- **Spatial growth:** crafted keys and utilities open sectors previously inaccessible.
- **Social growth:** faction contracts reward specific synthesis outcomes, raising status and influence.
- **Meta growth:** discovered component patterns can seed future player-created levels with internally coherent resource logic.

Without synthesis, exploration remains episodic. With synthesis, exploration becomes cumulative.

## Practical Implementation Priorities
1. Ensure each expedition yields at least one meaningful synthesis decision.
2. Gate major depth jumps behind mixed requirements (materials + reputation + route intel).
3. Keep failure states costly enough to create tension, but recoverable enough to sustain experimentation.
4. Build clear UI language around Access Tiers, Weave inputs, and output categories.

## Expansion Hooks
- Add sector-specific synthesis schools tied to faction doctrine.
- Introduce rare Weave anomalies that alter recipe behavior.
- Allow endgame players to publish curated sector templates for community-built levels.
