# Development Roadmap

## Purpose
This roadmap organizes production goals from first playable demo to long-term world growth, keeping narrative, systems, and community expansion aligned.

## Phase 1 — Playable Demo (Foundation Vertical Slice)
**Goal:** prove the core expedition-to-synthesis loop in a constrained but compelling slice.

### Scope
- One Anchor hub with basic services.
- Limited Maze region with layered paths and distinct hazards.
- Baseline expedition flow: prepare, explore, extract, synthesize, re-enter.
- Initial Access Tier progression (at least two tiers).
- Introductory faction contracts from at least two major factions.

### Success Criteria
- Players understand the loop without external explanation.
- Each run yields meaningful upgrade choices.
- Progression pace feels motivating across multiple sessions.
- Tone and world framing establish mystery without confusion.

## Phase 2 — System Expansion (Mid-Scale Play)
**Goal:** broaden strategic depth and social dynamics while preserving loop clarity.

### Scope
- Multiple Anchors with differentiated services and economies.
- Expanded sector variety (environmental patterns, risk profiles, encounter types).
- Deeper synthesis trees with specialization pathways.
- Full faction set introduced with competing incentives.
- Reputation and consequence systems tied to Strata and Access Tiers.

### Success Criteria
- Different playstyles (Pathfinder, Synthesist, Broker-oriented) are all viable.
- Faction choices produce visible short- and mid-term consequences.
- Deeper sectors feel qualitatively different, not just numerically harder.
- World systems remain readable as complexity increases.

## Phase 3 — Long-Term World Growth (Persistent Frontier)
**Goal:** transition from authored progression to a living world framework with player co-authorship.

### Scope
- High-tier political arcs that can alter regional access norms.
- Advanced Weave systems, anomalies, and rare synthesis doctrines.
- Seasonal or chapter-based world events affecting route stability.
- Infrastructure for future player-created levels/sectors with curation rules.
- Governance tooling for integrating community-built spaces into canon pathways.

### Success Criteria
- Endgame progression remains meaningful beyond content completion.
- Community-created spaces align with core systems and lore tone.
- Social hierarchy and faction politics stay dynamic under new content.
- Live-world operations can scale without eroding design coherence.

## Cross-Phase Production Principles
- Maintain strict terminology consistency (Maze, Weave, Strata, Access Tiers, Anchors, Expeditions).
- Preserve the exploration → synthesis → access progression spine.
- Tie narrative reveals to systemic milestones, not isolated cutscenes.
- Build every new feature with long-term world growth and community integration in mind.
