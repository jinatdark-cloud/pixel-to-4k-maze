# Maze: Pixel to 4K

A semi-open-world maze adventure game where exploration, growth, synthesis, and player-created levels gradually expand a living world.

## Overview

**Maze: Pixel to 4K** is a game project built around one core idea:

> the maze is not just a dungeon, but the foundation of civilization itself.

In this world, resources, power, technology, social status, and even access to truth are all tied to how deep one can go into the maze.  
Players begin at the lowest level of society and push forward through exploration, combat, discovery, and progression, gradually unlocking both new regions and new ways to shape the world.

This project is being developed step by step, starting from a playable demo and growing toward a larger semi-open-world structure.  
In the long run, the world is intended to include not only official levels, but also player-designed levels that can become part of the wider game ecosystem.

---

## Core Pillars

- **Maze-centered worldbuilding**  
  The maze is the economic, political, and spiritual center of the world.

- **Semi-open-world progression**  
  Players are free to explore, grow, and choose paths within a guided world structure.

- **Layered character growth**  
  Advancement is tied to deeper exploration, stronger survival ability, and higher access rights.

- **Synthesis-driven discovery**  
  Items, materials, systems, and progression can be built through combination and experimentation.

- **From official content to player contribution**  
  The long-term vision includes a world where players can create and contribute their own levels.

---

## World Premise

The world is divided by invisible but brutal hierarchy.

At the bottom are those who can barely survive the outer edges of the maze.  
Above them are the explorers, hunters, guild agents, and enforcers who profit from each deeper layer.  
At the top are those who control knowledge, routes, permissions, and the interpretation of what the maze truly is.

For most people, the maze is opportunity.  
For others, it is a prison.  
For a few, it may be the only path to rewriting the order of the world.

The player begins with almost nothing and steps into this system from the lowest level, seeking survival first, then strength, then truth.

## Gameplay Direction

The project is currently being designed around the following gameplay loop:

1. Enter the maze  
2. Explore, avoid danger, or fight enemies  
3. Collect resources, materials, and hidden clues  
4. Grow stronger through progression and synthesis  
5. Unlock new zones, systems, and higher-risk routes  
6. Return better prepared and challenge deeper layers

The first demo will focus on a **small but replayable maze structure** with:
- controllable randomness
- a guaranteed completable route
- meaningful enemy or encounter pressure
- clear progression feedback
- the foundation for future expansion

## Development Goals

### Phase 1 — Playable Demo
Build a small, coherent maze experience that proves the core loop:
- exploration
- pressure
- progression
- replayability

### Phase 2 — System Expansion
Expand the prototype into a stronger game framework with:
- deeper progression
- more layered world logic
- richer encounter design
- stronger identity and tone

### Phase 3 — Living World Direction
Move toward a broader ecosystem where:
- official stages establish the world
- player-made stages can be integrated over time
- the maze becomes a shared space shaped by both design and discovery

## Long-Term Vision

This is not intended to be only a simple maze game.

The long-term goal is to build a world where:
- the maze has systemic meaning
- progression feels tied to both gameplay and social status
- players can experience both authored and evolving content
- the project can grow from a small prototype into a much larger interactive world

From pixel-scale beginnings to a broader 4K vision, the project is designed to grow in layers — mechanically, visually, and narratively.
